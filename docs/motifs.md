# Motifs

Motifs are TND's fluid solution to skills. Characters have traits that make
them excel in certain areas, whether through practise, innate talent or
meticulous academic study. Their motifs reflect their personality, and make
checks easier.


## Bonuses

Once a character has gained a motif (which typically start with a bonus
of 1), you should note it down with an artistic name (required) and 
short description agreed with the DM.

Checks which fall into the motif's purview - which need not be limited
to a single stat - have the motif's bonus apply to them as a **modifier**.

Motifs can be improved to have higher bonuses up to **5**, as handed out
by the DM.


## Example motifs

The idea behind motifs representing "character traits" is best explained through
example, and what better example than the very inspiration for this RPG?

**Electrochemistry** is a skill in Disco Elysium, and would be a good motif in TND.
Although on the surface it represents the character's drug addiction, it's also
associated more generally with impulsive behaviour and the "party life", which gives
it a much wider purview than.. roll to sniff cocaine. 

Ensuring that a motif is central
to a character's theme while also practical and frequently utilisible is the key to making
it relevant in TND gameplay.
