# DM's guide

As a relatively DM-reliant TTRPG, it is important that you as the DM Know What
You Are Doing. Certain rules are made with the assumption that you know how to
play the system. Here's how you play the system.


## Stats, motifs and characters

Don't be too eager to dish out improvements; they are permanent, and poor judgement
will impact the longevity and balance of your game. That's all there is to say
on the matter.


## Checks

TND is a hard game. Checks are difficult, and when starting out, even Easy checks
can be daunting to a character that isn't specced into the matching archetype.
This is intentional. Failing checks should not be catastrophic; both outcomes
should be significant to the story without denying access to the narrative.

Here is a copy of the check value table. The difficulty descriptors are
rather accurate: Easy could be picked up by a beginner, but not by a complete
stranger to the job; Forte requires legendary capability to attain consistently.

Difficulty | Value | Default chance
---------- | ----- | --------------
Easy       | 8     | 58%
Reasonable | 10    | 28%
Difficult  | 12    | 8%
Impressive | 14    | 3% (critical)
Forte      | 16    | 3% (critical)


## Combat

Combat is dynamic. It shouldn't consist of back-and-forths of choosing
outcomes and making checks. Factor in the movement of players; ask
them where they are positioned (even using a diagram as illustration!)
and help them consider how they would use the environment around them.

As a system focused on roleplay, combat should be acted out, not
calculated. HP is absent because direct hits should be calculated
narrative decisions, not one more attack in a Pokemon battle.
