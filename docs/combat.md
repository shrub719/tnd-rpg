# Combat

You'll find that TND combat is not heavily mechanically governed. This leaves
much up to the imagination of the DM and the players, so understanding the flow
of combat is important.


## Key

When entering combat, you select a key - a single stat which you will use for the
**entire encounter**. Your current key determines what stat you use for attack checks.
You can only use leitmotifs under that stat for the encounter.

Your **defense** is the passive check value for your key stat.


## Turn order

Combat begins with the player (or enemy!) who initiated the encounter, decided by the DM.
From then, each player decides the next player to take their turn in a **popcorn system**.

Once all players have had their turn, the DM describes the actions of the opposing side,
and the cycle repeats.


## Turns

During a turn, you can take one of 3 actions.

- **Attack.** Make a check with your key stat against a single enemy's defense.
- **Interact.** Any action which attempts to use the environment or any items is
an interact action. You may need to make a check, and useful interactions can
result in modifiers for you or others.
- **Leitmotif.** Use a leitmotif under the right conditions. Described below.


## Attacks

TND has no hit points or similar mechanics. Instead, attacks significantly alter the
status of an enemy or player - they are difficult to hit, but rewarding.

If you are attacked, the DM must make a roll on the enemy's behalf against your
**defense**. Hits will result in negative effects on your character, from a change
in position or status to disabling certain actions or putting you out of commission
entirely.


## Leitmotifs

A leitmotif is an application of a motif that can be seen as a single "move" or ability.
You gain them throughout your journey - they come with certain conditions and special
effects.

They can be anything from special attacks to bonuses and modifier-activating abilities.
You can make them together with your DM.
